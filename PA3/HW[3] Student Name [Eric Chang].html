Problem Definition

We want to devlop an AI that can play and consistently win a game of 4x4x4 Tic-Tac-Toe. In order to do this we need to implement our AI using minimax with alpha-beta pruning and heuristics value assignments to give it the best chance on winning near 100% of the time. This result is useful as we would be able to determine that these algorithms can significantly outdo the random AI algorithm in a game of Tic-Tac-Toe. I assume that we would be able to come out with the results we expect, but with some difficulties on getting it to be very near 100%.

Method and Implementation

We utilized minimax with heuristics value assignemnt to future board states to solve our problem. Below will describe how each help solve our problem and how they were developed (pseudocode):

  • Minimax with alpha-beta pruning: This is a recursive algorithm used to help the AI determine what the best move to react to the other player's move, or in this case the other AI's move. By determining your oponent's most optimal move and assinging that the minimum value and giving you the most opimal move to counter. And we implemented alpha beta pruning by adding the parameters alpha and beta into the minimax. And made alpha = max(alpha, best) and if (beta <= alpha) then terminate, and then beta = min(beta, best) and terminate if beta <= alpha. We used the helper function minimax to accomplish this.
  • Heuristics: This is used to give future board states found by the AIs a value to assist the minmax function determine the most optimal choice. We loop through every possible 4 in a row winning outcome, and if there is a 4 in a row for the Max player the score gets +10, and if it is 4 in a row for the Min player then the score gets -10. We used the helper function labled evaluate to calculate and assign the heuristic values.

    Discussion

    Discuss your method and results:

    • Strengths are that it can continuously win against a random AI, as it seems to brute force placing down 4 in a row pieces. However it struggles to play against itself in the sense that it does not put enough emphasis on blocking the other's moves to prevent it from winning and instead just focuses on winning on its own.
    • Results show that it is very successful against random as in our 100 tests it never lost and had an average response time of 8.74ms/move. Slightly lower than the 9.98ms/move of Ai vs Ai as the random ai choice most likely took less time than our AI's decision making
    • The results of AI vs AI, it struggled to actually block the other player to prevent the other player from winning so for whoever goes first in our AI vs itself will win every time. Furthermore in the AI vs itself, in the 100 tests, every single test the Ais took the same moves except for who goes first and as a result which player was able to complete 4 in a row first. This was most likely due to a not fully fleshed out heuristic values assignment strategy, as it only took in to account for the four in a row board states and put no value on a favorable board state of 2 or 3 in a row pieces.
    • In future work what we can do is improve our heuristic values by giving more values for when there are two in a row or three in a row to help the AI determine when to block the other player's move.

    Conclusions

    Our conclusion is that our AI is very good at being able to defeat a random AI, however against a stronger AI it struggles to determine when it needs to block and what to do when it is blocked. It also does not react well to getting blocked. This could be because we did not use a while loop in our implemntation and we did not put the scores into an array that it could fall back on if a path got blocked.
